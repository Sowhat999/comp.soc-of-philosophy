Curtis Guy Yarvin (born 1973,[20][unreliable source?] better known by his pen name Mencius Moldbug, is an American computer scientist, political theorist, and neoreactionary thinker. He is the creator of the Urbit computing platform[21][unreliable source?] and the author of the blog Unqualified Reservations.

His writings have played a foundational role in the formation of the neoreactionary movement.[22] He borrowed the word formalism from the concept of legal formalism to describe his political philosophy of insisting on the alignment of property rights with political power.[5] The label "neo-reactionary" was applied to Yarvin's philosophy by Arnold Kling in 2010 and adopted by Yarvin's followers.[4]

Controversy[edit]

His opinions have been the subject of numerous controversies. In 2009, Patri Friedman rescinded his invitation to speak at a conference hosted by the The Seasteading Institute after he accused another speaker, Paul Romer, of intellectual dishonesty.[23][unreliable source?][non-primary source needed] In 2015, his invitation to speak about Urbit at the Strange Loop conference was rescinded following complaints made by other attendees.[24] In 2016, his invitation to the LambdaConf conference resulted in the cancellation of the PrlConf and Typelevel Summit[25] subconferences and the withdrawal of sponsors.[26]
