Apollonius (Ancient Greek: Απολλώνιος) of Chalcedon was a man of ancient Greece who taught philosophy. He was invited by the Roman emperor Antoninus Pius to come to Rome, for the purpose of instructing his son Marcus in philosophy.[1][2][3][4]

He was also possibly from Chalcis instead of Chalcedon, or, according to Cassius Dio, from Nicomedia.[5]
