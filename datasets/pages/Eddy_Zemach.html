Eddy M. Zemach (born in Jerusalem) is Ahad Ha'am Professor Emeritus in the Department of Philosophy at the Hebrew University of Jerusalem.[1] He received his Ph.D. from Yale University in 1965. His main research interests are aesthetics, metaphysics, epistemology, philosophy of psychology, and philosophy of language.

Major works[edit]

  • 1970: Analytic Aesthetics, Daga (in Hebrew).
  • 1976: Aesthetics, Institute for Poetics & Semiotics (in Hebrew).
  • 1992: The Reality of Meaning and the Meaning of 'Reality', Brown U. Press.[2]
  • 1992: Types: Essays in Metaphysics, E. J. Brill Publishers.
  • 1997: Real Beauty, Penn State Press.[3][4][5][6][7][8]
  • 2001: Mind and Right, Magnes Press (in Hebrew).
