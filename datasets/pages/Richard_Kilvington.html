Richard Kilvington (c. 1302-1361) was an English scholastic philosopher at the University of Oxford. His surviving works are lecture notes from the 1320s and 1330s. He was a Fellow of Oriel College, Oxford.[1] He was involved in a controversy over the nature of the infinite, with Richard FitzRalph, of Balliol College.[2]

In the 1340s he worked for Richard of Bury, bishop of Durham.[3]
